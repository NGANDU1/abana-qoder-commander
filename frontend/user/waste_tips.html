Waste Disposal & Recycling Tips

Learn how to properly dispose of different types of waste and maximize recycling.

Recycling Guidelines

Paper & Cardboard
  • Clean, dry, and flattened
  • No food contamination
  • No wax-coated cardboard
  • Remove plastic windows from envelopes
Plastics
  • Rinse containers before recycling
  • Remove caps and lids
  • Check local guidelines for #1-#7 plastics
  • No plastic bags or film
Glass
  • Clean and dry
  • No broken glass in recycling bins
  • Separate by color if required
  • No ceramics or window glass
Metals
  • Rinse cans and containers
  • No scrap metal in curbside bins
  • Aluminum foil must be clean
  • Remove plastic components

Composting Guide

What to Compost
  • Fruit and vegetable scraps
  • Coffee grounds and filters
  • Eggshells
  • Yard trimmings and leaves
  • Paper towels (unbleached)
What NOT to Compost
  • Meat, fish, and dairy products
  • Fats, oils, and grease
  • Pet waste and litter
  • Weeds with seeds
  • Coal or charcoal ash

Hazardous Waste Disposal

Important Safety Information

Hazardous waste requires special handling. Never pour down drains, throw in regular trash, or burn hazardous materials.

Batteries
  • Drop off at electronics retailers
  • Recycling centers accept all battery types
  • Never throw in regular trash
Electronics
  • Many retailers offer take-back programs
  • Recycling centers accept computers, phones, TVs
  • Data wipe before disposal
Chemicals
  • Hazardous waste collection events
  • Home improvement stores accept paint
  • Auto parts stores accept motor oil

Waste Reduction Tips

Reduce
  • Use reusable shopping bags
  • Choose products with minimal packaging
  • Buy in bulk to reduce packaging waste
  • Avoid single-use plastics
Reuse
  • Repair items instead of replacing
  • Donate usable items
  • Use refillable containers
  • Borrow tools and equipment
